Emergency Assistance Eligibility Wizard

The COVID-19 outbreak is causing extraordinary disruption for companies and non-profits across the Garden State. The NJEDA wants to make it easy for organizations to understand what support programs are available to you to help stabilize your operations and get back on a pathway to growth.

This is a beta version of the Eligibility Wizard that focuses on newly announced State Emergency Assistance programs and the SBA disaster loan program. We will be updating it regularly as new federal, state, local, and philanthropic programs become available.

Disclaimer: As a courtesy, the NJEDA is offering this screening tool (the “Eligibility Wizard”) that lists emergency assistance programs that you may decide to consider based upon information you provide about your organization.

The Eligibility Wizard neither approves nor denies eligibility for programs, nor does it reserve any funding or a place in any application queue. It is the responsibility of your organization to fully assess your eligibility for any State or Federal assistance program. The full specifications for each State emergency assistance program are located on the COVID Business Information Hub: https://cv.business.nj.gov. To apply for a program listed below, you need to apply through the appropriate administering agency. The administering agency will determine all eligibility requirements upon your submission of an application to that agency for the respective program.

Emergency Assistance Grant Program

Description: An operating expense grant for NJ-based businesses or non-profits with 1-10 full time employees in the retail/accommodation/food service/arts/entertainment/recreation/service industries.

Eligible Uses: Operating expense support to help stabilize operations and reduce furloughs or layoffs for companies that can demonstrate they have been adversely impacted by the Covid-19 emergency.

Available Funding: $1K per full-time employee, up to $5,000.

Learn more here: https://faq.business.nj.gov/en/articles/3835237

Emergency Assistance Loan Program

Description: A no/low-cost loan for up to 10 years for NJ-based businesses with under $5M in revenue.

Eligible Uses: Supports business continuity to help stabilize operations and reduce furloughs or layoffs for companies that can demonstrate they have been adversely impacted by the Covid-19 emergency.

Available Funding: Up to $100,000 of working capital loans for up to 10 years. 0% interest rate (years 1-5), NJEDA’s prevailing interest rate floor (capped at 3.0% years 6-10).

Learn more here: https://faq.business.nj.gov/en/articles/3835238

Emergency Assistance Guarantee Program

Description: This program is supported by NJEDA but administered by Premier Lenders or Community Development Finance Institutions. This program provides a guarantee to Premier Lenders or Premier CDFIs that will issue working capital loans or letters of credit to support business continuity for a range of COVID-19 related impacts to businesses (reduced revenue, employee shortage, supply chain impact, etc.)

Eligible Uses: Covers operating expenses for New Jersey small businesses and non-profit organizations that have been impacted by the COVID-19 outbreak.

Available Funding: Benefits include a 50% guarantee of working capital loans, with eligibility for up to a $100K working capital loan; the program can provide up to $10 million in total.

Learn more here: https://faq.business.nj.gov/en/articles/3835236

Entrepreneur Guarantee Program

Description: Program provides 80% loan guarantees for working capital loans to start-up entrepreneurs, encouraging private capital to flow to start-up companies and providing positive reinforcement to a cautious investor marketplace.

Eligible Uses: Investors can use this program to provide new funding to NJ entrepreneurial businesses in which they are already an investor.

Available Funding: There is a $200,000 company exposure cap, with up to $5 million available.

Learn more here: https://faq.business.nj.gov/en/articles/3835239

Financial relief on existing NJEDA obligations

Description: A series of steps to support existing NJEDA business customers, including a 3-month payment moratorium, waived late fees on loan repayments late fees and/or loan modification fees, and allowing collateral releases, subordinations and substitutions on business assets.

Eligible Uses: Support for current NJEDA business customers

Availability: Current NJEDA business customers. Existing NJEDA business customers should reach out to their Servicing Officer for more information.

Additional financing options that you may be eligible for:

Loans from a CDFI

Community Development Financial Institutions (CDFIs) play a critical role in providing access to financing tools and resources to businesses and individuals that have difficulty accessing the traditional banking sector. Specifically, the NJEDA has created two programs (https://faq.business.nj.gov/en/articles/3835240), the CDFI Emergency Assistance Grant Program and the CDFI Loan Loss Reserve Fund to help local CDFI’s scale their lending to micro and small businesses, especially in communities and business segments that are traditionally underserved.

You can reach out directly to a CDFI to see if you would be eligible for any of their State-supported and non-State supported financing programs. Click the link above to see a list of New Jersey-based CDFIs that focus on micro and small business financing.

Financing from a private bank

Often one of the most reliable sources of financing for micro, small, and medium-sized businesses is their traditional business banking partner. Many banks in New Jersey work with the NJEDA to provide low cost financing to micro, small, and medium-sized enterprises. You can contact your local banking branch for more information.

Federal Small Business Administration (SBA) Emergency Injury Disaster Loans (EIDL)

Description: Provides low-interest federal disaster loans for working capital to small businesses suffering substantial economic injury as a result of the Coronavirus (COVID-19).

Eligible Uses: Working capital

Availability: Loans are administered by the federal government. Businesses are advised to submit applications as soon as possible.

Learn more here: https://disasterloan.sba.gov/ela

Federal Economic Injury Disaster Loan Advance

Description: Provides emergency advance of up to $10,000 to small businesses and private non-profits harmed by COVID-19 within 3 days of applying for an SBA Economic Injury Disaster Loan (EIDL). This loan advance will not have to be repaid.

Eligible Uses: May be used to keep employees on payroll, to pay for sick leave, meet increased production costs due to supply chain disruptions, or pay business obligations, including debts, rent and mortgage payments.

Availability: Loans are administered by the federal government. Businesses must first apply for an EIDL.

Learn more here: https://www.sba.gov/page/coronavirus-covid-19-small-business-guidance-loan-resources

Federal 7(a) Loan

Description: Federal Small Business Administration works with lenders to provide up to $5 million to small businesses. The SBA will guarantee up to 85 percent of loans of $150,000 or less and 75 percent of loans of $150,000 or above.

Eligible Uses: Loans may be used to expand, acquire, or start a business, purchase or construct real estate, refinance existing business debt, buy equipment, provide working capital, purchase inventory.

Availability: The specific terms of the SBA loans are negotiated between the borrower and the participating lender.

Learn more here: https://www.sba.gov/partners/lenders/7a-loan-program

Paycheck Protection Program (PPP) Loans

Description: Provides cash-flow assistance through 100 percent federally guaranteed loans to employers who maintain their payroll during this emergency. If employers maintain their payroll, the loans would be forgiven.

Eligible Uses: May be used to keep employees on payroll, pay for vacation, parental, family, medical or sick leave, pay for dismissal or separation, pay for group health care benefits, retirement benefits, or pay for State or local tax assessed on the compensation of employees.

Availability: Loans are administered by eligible SBA 7(a) lenders. SBA has a free referral service tool called Lender Match to help find a lender near you.

Learn more here: https://www.sba.gov/page/coronavirus-covid-19-small-business-guidance-loan-resources

Small Business Debt Relief Program

Description: Provides immediate relief to small businesses with non-disaster SBA loans, in particular 7(a), 504, and microloans. Under it, SBA will cover all loan payments on these SBA loans, including principal, interest, and fees, for six months. This relief will also be available to new borrowers who take out loans within six months of the President signing the bill into law.

Eligible Uses: Relief from non-disaster SBA loans.

Availability: Relief is available immediately to those who hold a non-disaster SBA loan and to those who take out a 7(a), 504, or microloan within six months of the President signing the bill into law.

Learn more here: https://www.sba.gov/page/coronavirus-covid-19-small-business-guidance-loan-resources

Counseling & Training

Description: Additional funds provided to resource partners and associations to expand their reach and better support small business owners with counseling and up-to-date information regarding COVID-19.

Resources:

Exiting Wizard

The State of New Jersey COVID-19 Business Support Eligibility Wizard is designed to help organizations registered in New Jersey identify emergency assistance programs for which they are eligible.

You can read about all of our programs at https://cv.business.nj.gov/

If your business or non-profit is not registered to do business in New Jersey, you are not eligible for NJEDA emergency assistance programs, however, please consider seeking support from the following entities:

  • Your state’s economic development agency
  • Your local county or municipality’s economic development agency
  • A private bank that may have support programs in your area
  • Your local Community Development Financial Institution (CDFI)
  • The federal Small Business Administration’s Economic Injury Disaster Loan (EIDL)- https://disasterloan.sba.gov/ela/
